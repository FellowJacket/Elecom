Elecoms Archival Corporation

Securing the future, today.

Facilitating the cultural integrity of mankind

Since 1976, Elecoms Archival Corporation has prided itself on guaranteeing the preservation of cultural and historic mediums. Beginning as a small hardware company in the mid-1970s, Electronic Communications Ltd. soon became the world-changing and revolutionary corporation it is today.

Why we archive

Not for centuries, has humanity posessed the technology and capability to destroy itself. The invention of Thermonuclear Bombs has not only put the probability of losing billions of human lives, but the very foundation of our cultures; History. We began in the early 1980's preserving historical commons, like critically acclaimed literature, and film. Soon, we realized that it would simply not be enough. Our mission took us to preserve even more. Historical footage, texts, and audio files have all been, with the help of new technology, preserved.

"The future is uncertain. The preservation of history, shouldn't be."
- Wylan Redd, CEO and founder of Elecoms

Our facilities

With just over two dozen locations inside of this great country, Elecoms is continually growing. Consisting of 6 COM-Class server centers, 5 OLS-J Distribution facilities, and 18 FOA reinforced underground storage facilities.
